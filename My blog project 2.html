My First day in UdeM

Posted on 1 September 2021

One keyword : difference. Everything is different. Let me explain.

Before I become an actual University dude, I was in something called "cegep". Cegep is a place that is between high school and university. It treats their students like high schoolers, but gives them the freedom of work of a university student. In cegep, the teachers have more ability to approach their students personally and be patient with their misunderstandings and their questions. However, Uni teachers seem to not give a single fuck about what their students think of them. And that's totally fair, in my opinion, considering the fact that they probably have a double amount of students to look after and a bigger pile of work to do and bigger responsabilities in university and outside university too.

Considerning that I'm a computer science student, I'll give an example from my first day in the computer science field 😄 On my first day, I started with a Calculus 1 class. To start off, the teacher's rhythm was unbelievable! He would change the subject very fast and wouldn't take much time to see who really understood and who didn't. But again, I understand that they're uni teachers and bla bla bla bla, but I never expected it to be THAT fast. I guess I have to adapt.

My First week in UdeM

Posted on 7 September 2021

One week has passed since my first day in university! It felt a little weird to be finally able to call myself a university student after all the issues that I've been through in highschool and cegep, from self-doubt to low grades. It was one hell of a rollercoaster. However, after all of that, here I am, a student in one of the top 100 universities in the world, in one of the most paid/in demand programs at the moment.

Enough deep thoughts, let's focus on what I saw/learnt during the first week. My impression about my university until now remains the same : the difference makes it a little hard to adapt, but I'm sure we'll get through it. Something that surprised me is that there are way more international students than I thought there will be. In fact, most of the people that I've talked to until now are international students! However, their presence reminds me of one of the things I want to do while I'm in university : Be and exchange student. Yes, I want to travel the world while I'm studying. I want to see new places, new cultures, new streets, new faces. It would be refreshing and also very amusing.

My second week in UdeM

Posted on 14 September 2021

Here we are, suffering all together. It has been hell until now! The amount of knowledge I have to integrate into my mind, especially that it's coming from 5 different subjects in which I have to pass them all with an average of at least 75% in all of them.

Why? Well, in my university there is an orientation called "COOP" in which the student that wants to go in will have the opportunity of getting 3 internships instead of the one that is permitted to you in the general orientation. COOP will help me not only gain some extra experience, but it'll also help me in creating a lot of connections and friendships that could potentially help me in my future and my career.

That is why I'll try my best to work hard. I mean, creating this blog from scratch is already a part of the hardwork process since I had no clue what even is HTML, CSS, JS, etc. My biggest experience in programming was to create this game from scratch on Vpython in Cegep. Although I didn't do much in the project since it was my friend who did the most of it since he was more knowledgeable than me, but it still counts as relevant experience since I did learn a few things on it. I trust myself and the process because I know it'll be hard in the beginning, but with some hardwork I'll be able to do anything and create whatever I want.
